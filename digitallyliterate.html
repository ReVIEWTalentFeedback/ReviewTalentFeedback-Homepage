Evaluator Capacity

The ReVIEW Talent Feedback System is built on Revision Learning's Six characteristics of effective evaluators

Any University that provides supervision of online learning will find ReVIEW invaluable. We can provide online instructors with training and evaluation. This ensures all supervisors have calibration against a framework of your choice. It allows teachers to growth through effective feedback.

  • Rubric Understanding

    Online Instructors need to understand the current teaching frameworks used by your university and accrediting bodies.

  • Qualitative and Quantitative Feedback

    Effective teaching is so much more than tagged evidence. The ReVIEW Talent Feedback System encourages online teachers and their supervisors to use both qualitative and quantitative evidence aligned to indicators developed in your framework.

  • Curriculum, Instruction, and Assessment

    Instructional capacity increases when teachers get actionable feedback based on best pedagogical practices. The ReVIEW Talent Feedback system coaches supervisors of online instructors through the Claim, Connect, Action method to ensure teachers focus on meaningful student engagement and learning.

  • Supportive Yet Critical Feedback

    When educators receive feedback for growth directly connected to your indicators instructional capacity grows. The ReVIEW Talent Feedback System puts the focus on coaching and development not tagging of evidence.

  • An Awareness of Bias

    We all have different lenses. The ReVIEW Talent Feedback System helps supervisors of online instructors recognize their bias and provide the most objective feedback possible.

  • Clear Communication

    Our system train supervisors of online instructors to write effective feedback reports that are evidence driven. Using the Claim, Connect, Action method evaluators learn to make claims based on a framework, connect that claim to observed evidence, and then recommend actionable feedback.

Faculty Evaluations

Course surveys in online teaching are notoriously unreliable. We believe creating an evlaution plan based on a growth model will serve faculty more than any Likert scale. In ReVIEW Talent Feedback System users are organized by their roles and postions.

ReVIEW Talent Feedback System allows you to choose between Teacher, Facilitator, Evaluator, and School Admin. Each role increases in rights. Users can have multiple roles.

  • Teacher: Submits artifacts and can be evaluated. Does not evaluate anyone else.
  • Evaluator: Can evaluate a teacher.
  • Facilitator: Provides feedback to the evaluator
  • School Admin: Can access all completed forms in that school, can change evaluator and teacher assignments.

Group Management

In ReVIEW Talent Feedback System the frequency of events and observations depends on group assignments. Department Chairs can create unlimited groups and assign forms and frequencies based on group membership.

First you make a group. You can then assign a user to the group by access the school management window.

You can then select events for each group.

Educator Preparation

Engineering Better Teachers

ReVIEW Talent Feedback System is designed with the goal of improving teacher preparation. Schools of Education in rural communities use ReVIEW's video upload to observe remote teachers. Candidates across the country find value in being scored using the same frameworks they will use in the classroom.

The ReVIEW Talent Management System is the only comprehensive solution for States who wish to track teacher candidates beyond student teaching.

We offer training for educator and calibration of your student teaching supervisors. We also offer expert scoring of student observations. Student teachers deserve highly trained and calibrated supervisors. While most universities and colleges use retired teachers and administrators who bring years of experience. Yet as evaluators they receieve little to no training. Using our library of normed videos educator preparation programs offer supervisors professional development while also making sure they have the necessary skillset to evaluate student teachers.
